Common Questions
How long do I have to respond to a debt lawsuit in NY?
20 days if served in person. 30 days if served by mail or substituted service. The clock starts the day you were served — not the day you opened the papers. If you are not sure when the clock started, assume 20 days and act immediately.
What happens if I ignore a debt lawsuit?
The plaintiff's attorney files for a default judgment. A judge signs it without any hearing. Once entered, they can freeze your bank account, garnish your wages, and report the judgment to credit bureaus — all without any additional court action.
Can I fight a debt lawsuit even if I owe the money?
Yes. Filing an Answer forces the plaintiff to prove the debt — the amount, that they own it, and that it is within the statute of limitations. Many debt collection cases are dismissed because the collector cannot produce the original contract or proof of ownership. Filing an Answer gives you leverage to negotiate a settlement at a fraction of the claimed amount.
What is the statute of limitations on debt in NY?
3 years for most consumer debt in New York as of 2022. If the debt is older than 3 years from your last payment, it may be time-barred and you can raise this as a defense in your Answer. Never acknowledge an old debt in writing — it can restart the clock.
