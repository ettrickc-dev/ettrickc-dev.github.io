Common Questions
How do I know if my NY divorce is final?
Your divorce is legally final when a judge signs the Judgment of Divorce and it is entered by the County Clerk. On eCourts, your case will show as "Disposed." A case that is still "Active" or "Pending" is not finalized — you are still legally married.
How long does a NY divorce take?
An uncontested divorce in New York typically takes 3 to 6 months after all paperwork is filed correctly. Contested divorces can take 1 to 3 years. In NYC courts, processing times are often longer due to court volume. If your case has been "Active" for more than 6 months with no movement, something may be missing.
What if my name doesn't show up?
Two reasons: the case was never filed, or it was filed very recently and hasn't been assigned to a judge yet. eCourts only shows cases that have been assigned. Very new filings may not appear for a few weeks. You can also check directly with your county clerk.
Can I file for divorce myself in NY without a lawyer?
Yes. New York allows uncontested divorces to be filed without an attorney when both parties agree on all terms. The NY Courts provide the forms for free.
